Chop 'N' Swap Shop

I wrote this about the NPL flat type Chop 'N' Swap, which weirdly I can't find on the online guide to the Enigma. Here's the description:

In a chop ‘n’ swap, a letter is removed (chopped) from somewhere in the middle of a word or phrase; the two pieces remaining switch positions (swap). Examples: shame → chop the “a” → swap the parts → mesh; and ta(f)fies

Hope that helps! If you're new to flats as a puzzle type, check out this page for some examples. An important note is that the poems don't necessarily rhyme/have the same meter after you replace the capitalized words!
